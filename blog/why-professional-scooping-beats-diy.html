The Hidden Cost of "Doing It Yourself"

We've all been there. You spend your Saturday afternoon dodging "landmines" in the backyard instead of enjoying a BBQ or playing with the kids. While scooping your own yard might seem like a simple chore, there are hidden costs in time, hygiene, and health that most homeowners don't consider.

1. Reclaim Your Weekend

The average dog owner spends 30-45 minutes a week on yard cleanup. That adds up to over 30 hours a year! By hiring Clean Feet, you're not just buying a clean yard; you're buying back your time. Imagine never having to worry about who's turn it is to "scoop the poop" again.

2. Superior Sanitization

DIY cleanup usually involves a plastic bag or a simple rake-and-bin method. While this removes the visible waste, it doesn't address the bacteria and odors left behind. At Clean Feet, we use hospital-grade disinfectants that are safe for pets and children, ensuring your grass is truly fresh.

3. Consistency is Key

It's easy to skip a week when it's raining, snowing, or you're just too busy. But skipping even one week can lead to waste being trodden into the house or washed into the soil. Our uniformed pros show up rain or shine, ensuring a consistent perimeter sweep that keeps your yard pristine year-round.

4. Proper Waste Disposal

Did you know that pet waste is considered a pollutant by the EPA? Simply throwing it in your regular trash can lead to odors and attract pests. We haul the waste away for you and dispose of it according to local Omaha regulations, keeping your bins clean and your neighborhood fresh.

Conclusion
